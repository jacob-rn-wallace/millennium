# MX1000 mould markings reference

This document records mould markings observed on plastic case parts across all seven M-RAG97 project specimens. It covers identifier formats, per-specimen survey data, and open questions arising from the data. Physical inspection was performed by opening each mouse and reading markings moulded directly into each part.

---

## Identifier formats

Three distinct 2nd identifier formats have been observed across the specimen set. They do not appear to be interchangeable — each format correlates with a distinct group of parts and/or production context.

**CAV.x** — a cavity number in the standard injection moulding sense, indicating which cavity in a multi-cavity mould tool produced the part. Appears on parts that carry a Logitech part number (P/N), on Mice 3–7.

**SZ** (followed by a number, with or without a hyphen) — an identifier of unknown meaning. Appears on parts that carry a P/N on Mice 1 and 2, and on the battery indicator window of Mice 5–7. The significance of the SZ prefix is not confirmed; it may indicate a different moulding supplier, a different tool set, or a different marking convention. See open questions.

**Bare number** — a plain integer with no prefix. Appears on parts that do not carry a P/N (smaller parts where the marking area was too small for the full CAV format), and on the battery indicator window of Mice 1–4. Whether these are cavity numbers in the same sense as CAV.x identifiers is not confirmed.

### Format distribution by part type

| Part type | Identifier format |
|-----------|------------------|
| Parts with P/N, Mice 3–7 | CAV.x |
| Parts with P/N, Mice 1–2 | SZ |
| Parts without P/N | Bare number |
| Battery indicator window, Mice 1–4 | Bare number |
| Battery indicator window, Mice 5–7 | SZ |
| Scroll wheel surround, Mouse 1 | SZ |
| Scroll wheel surround, Mouse 3 | None observed |

The battery indicator window (402449-0000) is anomalous in that it carries a P/N but uses the bare number format on Mice 1–4 and the SZ format on Mice 5–7, rather than CAV.x on any specimen. The scroll wheel surround uses bare numbers on Mice 2–7, SZ on Mouse 1, and has no observed identifier on Mouse 3.

Mould markings were recorded from physical inspection of all seven specimens. Part numbers were read from markings moulded into each part. Entries marked — either bore no marking or the marking area was too small to read reliably.

## Observations and open questions

**The SZ format is not yet understood.** It may indicate a different moulding supplier, a different tool set, or an alternative marking convention used by the same supplier. The fact that Mice 1 and 2 use SZ on all their major structural parts while Mice 3–7 use CAV suggests the difference is at the shell level rather than the part level — consistent with a supplier or tooling change between production runs. Whether SZ encodes the same information as CAV (i.e. a cavity number) or something different is unknown.

**The bare number format on small parts may or may not be cavity numbers.** The values range 1–4 across all specimens and vary freely between mice with no obvious correlation to production date or batch. If they are cavity numbers they are consistent with small multi-cavity tools (up to 4 cavities), but the lack of pattern makes this uncertain.

**The battery indicator window straddles all three formats** — bare number on Mice 1–4, SZ on Mice 5–7 — despite carrying a P/N. This is unexplained. It may reflect a supplier change partway through the production run specifically for this part, or it may reflect something about how the part was sourced relative to the structural parts.

**The scroll wheel surround on Mouse 1 uses SZ rather than a bare number**, unlike all other specimens where it uses a bare number (or nothing, in the case of Mouse 3). Mouse 1 is also one of the two mice (along with Mouse 2) where the structural parts use SZ rather than CAV. Whether the surround's SZ marking on Mouse 1 is related to this broader SZ pattern for that shell, or coincidental, is not confirmed.

**The scroll wheel surround on Mouse 3 has no observed 2nd identifier**, making it the only part/specimen combination in the dataset with a confirmed absence. All other parts on Mouse 3 have identifiers. The reason for this absence is unknown.

---

## Parts not surveyed

The following parts were present in all specimens but yielded no identifier data, either because no marking was observed or because the marking area was inaccessible:

- Battery indicator light pipe
- Scroll wheel (rubberized; markings unlikely)
- Connect/Reset button
- Power switch

These are not flagged as open questions — absence of markings on very small or rubberized parts is expected.
